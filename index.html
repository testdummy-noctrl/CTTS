Step 1 – What income did you receive?
Based on your selections (and province), the relevant slip input panels below will appear. You can still override in the Income section.
T4 – Statement of Remuneration Paid
Copy amounts exactly from your T4. Key boxes: 14 (employment income), 16 (CPP/QPP contributions), 18 (EI premiums), 20 (RPP contributions), 22 (income tax deducted), 40 (taxable benefits). [web:83][web:91]
RL‑1 – Employment and other income (Québec)
For Québec employment income. Common boxes: A (employment income), B (QPP), C (EI), D (QPIP), E (Québec income tax). [web:86][web:92]
T4A / T4A(P) / T4A(OAS) – Pension, retirement, CPP/QPP, OAS
Use for pension income, RRIF, annuities, CPP/QPP benefits, Old Age Security. [web:76][web:79][web:84]
T4E – Employment Insurance and other benefits
T4E box 14 usually shows total EI benefits; box 22 shows income tax deducted. [web:84]
T5 – Statement of Investment Income
Common boxes: 13 (interest), 24 (eligible dividends), 25 (other than eligible dividends), 22 (tax deducted). [web:65][web:56][web:84]
RL‑3 – Investment income (Québec)
Use with T5 for Québec investment income; boxes cover interest, dividends and provincial tax withheld. [web:60]
T3 – Statement of Trust Income
Trust income allocations (interest, dividends, capital gains, etc.). [web:70][web:78]
T5007 – Statement of Benefits
Reports social assistance and workers’ compensation benefits which may enter net income and special lines. [web:83][web:81]
T5008 – Securities transactions
Use to determine proceeds and gains for capital gains reporting; you must also supply your adjusted cost base (ACB). [web:84]
T5013 – Partnership income
Limited partnership or other partnership allocations that flow into business and capital income. [web:84][web:81]
The slip wizard is for convenience only. Underlying mappings are approximate and may group some boxes together; always rely on actual slips and official CRA / Revenu Québec instructions when filing.
